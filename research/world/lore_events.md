# Key Lore Events — EvergeenNova

Timeline uses the campaign's in-world year numbering (Fourth Age).

---

## Fall of Arcadia (Year 276)

The Horned King — once benevolent, long since corrupted — ruled the island of Arcadia. A coalition of fey and mortals used the **Mirror of Aretas** to destroy him. The island sank. The Horned King's essence was not destroyed — it fragmented into the Antiseeds.

The Mirror of Aretas subsequently trapped the soul of **Bloody Mary**, a dangerous empress who had seized control of OXIE from Merlin.

---

## Birth of Briar Rose / Maleficent's Curse (Year 287–303)

Briar Rose (Aurora Plantagenet) was born in 287. When she turned 16 (year 303), Maleficent — archfey dragon and ruler of Pegaroth — cursed her. The nature of the curse required a deal: **Tobias Gold** (Memphis Cadwallader) brokered an arrangement that put Briar Rose to sleep for 300+ years instead of killing her. The favour owed to Cadwallader was left open.

---

## Strife of Camlin (Year ~300)

King Arthur attempted to use his sister Morgana in a blood ritual to produce a Pendragon heir. This caused the Strife of Camlin — a major political rupture between them. The ritual produced **Mordred** (born 336), a Changeling made from the blood of Arthur and Queen Guinevere through Morgana's magic.

---

## Morgana Severs the Avatar Bond (~Year 281)

Morgana Le Fay, then the avatar of the Three-Faced Lady, used Caliburn to cut the goddess out of herself. This reportedly killed or severely wounded the deity. The Thrice-Triple Covens' connection to their patron has been fractured ever since.

---

## Death of All-Mother Gothel (~Year 311)

Dame Gothel, the most powerful All-Mother the Cruthic Covens ever had, died (or disappeared) around year 311. This triggered the **All-Mother Succession Crisis** — years of infighting among the covens that left them weakened and fractured.

Morgana also first died in 311, in Oz.

---

## Queen Maeve's Coup (Year 323)

Queen Maeve seized control of Avalon from its previous ruler in 323. She has since built it into a military power now at war with Camelot for thirty years.

---

## Maleficent Discovers the Antiseeds (Year 343)

Maleficent, an archfey dragon with deep roots in old magic, discovered the Antiseeds — the fragmented essence of the Horned King — and brought this knowledge to the Silverheart Court. The plan to resurrect the Horned King began here.

---

## Beast King's Conquest (Year 346)

The Beast King, a Silverheart Court member, conquered Salasarglumm. Later defeated by the Mythmakers Union.

---

## Present Day (~Year 361)

The Seventh Sunderance is overdue and imminent. The Silverheart Court accelerates its plans. The Blight plague spreads through Arrnonland. The Redeye pandemic spreads from Xyander. Briar Rose has woken.

## The Counting Rhyme (Session 1)

A prophetic verse recited by the DM in Session 1. Each player rolled a d20 and received the corresponding line. The DM said the numbers would be "extremely important in about a year." The rhyme runs from 1 to 21, with each number tied to a mystical concept:

- 1: "Once upon a time"
- 4: (implied death-related)
- 5: "Five for silver"
- 6: "Six for gold"
- 7: "Seven for a secret never to be told"
- 8: "Eight for a tailed stars of sun, allowing for a gate"
- 9: "That cannot be undone"
- 10: "Ten for the trees of forgotten law"
- 11: "Eleven for a key to the spectral door"
- 12: "Twelve for a mirror that reflects the night"
- 13: "Thirteen for a beast that lives in spite"
- 14: "Fourteen for a world beyond our reach"
- 15: "Fifteen for a speech no tongue can teach"
- 16: "Sixteen for a dream trapped in stone"
- 17: "Seventeen for the old gods' hollow moon"
- 18: "Eighteen for the abyss that gazes back"
- 19: "Nineteen for the oncoming formless wreck"
- 20: "Twenty for a raven's final verse"
- 21: "And one more for a terrible unbreakable curse"

**Player rolls:** Rose=6, Ryan=19, Emma=11, others=15, 4, 1. The significance of each number has yet to be fully revealed but some lines clearly connect to campaign events (e.g. 11="key to the spectral door" for Drizella, 12="mirror that reflects the night" for Bloody Mary, 19="oncoming formless wreck" possibly the Sunderance).

## The Redeye Pandemic

A disease originating in Xyander (one of the largest cities). Symptoms: flu-like illness for a couple of weeks, then mental deterioration. All arrivals at Lowmarsh were screened for it in Session 1 via eye inspection at the gatehouse. Distinct from Ghoul Fever and the Blight plague.

## The Parallax

An astronomical alignment of the winter solstice and full moon, occurring during Sessions 2–3. During this event:
- Prophecy is at maximum power — "much more accurate, clear, and precise" than at any time in living memory
- Occult/cult spellcasters become significantly more powerful
- The Deer-Masked Stranger (Nimue) timed a heart-harvesting ritual to coincide with it
- Bessa of the Lone Pine told Sibylla this was the ideal time to bond with a patron

---

## Iron Mark / Iron-skin Trees

A material harvested from Iron-skin trees found deep in the Greenwoods. Harder and lighter than steel. Used in the handle/guard of Mordred's sword, which also bears a briar/feather pattern on the hilt and a "W" inscription on the pommel that Mordred tried to scratch off.

## The Shadic Brethren — First Appearance (Session 3)

The Shadic Brethren (also called "the Shadows") are a secretive monastic order serving the Faceless Soldier. During Session 3, a tall cloaked monk appeared before Drizella at midnight in the Lone Pine tavern and warned: "Something is coming. Many will survive... none unchanged. You do not wish to be here tonight." The figure then dissolved into shadow. The phrase "most will survive" was identified (via a religion check) as coming from the scriptures of the Faceless Soldier. This is the first direct interaction with the Brethren; their later actions include warning Drizella to flee before they assassinate Lewis Short.

---

## The Goodly Father — Church in Lowmarsh (Session 3)

The Immaculate Church of the Goodly Father is a major religious institution in Lowmarsh. The Goodly Father's domain covers civilization, law, and order — a common folk religion. The church is loosely ordered, led locally by Mother Mary Hill (healer) and Vicar Sean Rothwell (more magically skilled). The Faceless Soldier shares the same pantheon as the Goodly Father but represents an older, less institutional faith. Nik Jones reacted with visible distress to a portrait in the church connected to the entity Davy Jones.

---

## Thori Black's Chambers — Level 8 Arcane Illusion (Session 3)

Thori Black's personal quarters in the Lowmarsh treasury were found under a level 8 arcane illusion — the most powerful magic the party (excluding Mordred) had encountered. Papers contained meaningless filler text, objects flickered between states, and the desk surface showed inconsistent wood grain ("stitching"). The spell was cast approximately one week before discovery, coinciding with Thori's disappearance. His assistant also went missing at the same time. The vault lock proved too difficult even for a natural 20 on thievery.

---

<!-- Added from Session 4 -->

## Thori Black Illusion — Occult Not Arcane (Session 4)

Sibylla dispelled the illusion on Thori Black's apartment (natural 20 hero-point reroll). The spell was confirmed as **8th-level occult** (not arcane) magic, cast via a scroll or rite by a **fey creature** — not by a powerful caster directly. It was cast three weeks before discovery — two weeks before Thori officially "went missing." The apartment was ransacked but not fought in. His personal hygiene items were missing (suggesting he left voluntarily or was taken with basic possessions). A prescription medicine bottle was found.

---

## Hags vs. Witches (Session 4)

In this setting, a *witch* is a human spellcaster; a *hag* is a witch (or other human) who has been warped and transformed by overuse of magic, misuse of magic, or sustained magical exposure over a lifetime. Hags are "somewhere between human and fey, as well as some things beyond." The transformation corrupts both body and mind.

---

## Bessa of the Lone Pine Seeks Nimue (Session 4)

Bessa of the Lone Pine (one of Nimue's former students) approached Sibylla during a coven ritual near Lowmarsh. She revealed she has been searching for Nimue, who has been missing approximately twenty years. She gave Sibylla a sealed letter with an unrecognised heraldic wax seal and told her to "speak my name" to contact her. A blue-skinned hag also participated in the coven ritual, saying "hope for both" before teleporting away.

## Thori Black — Death Confirmed (Session 5)

Thori Black was found dead inside the Lowmarsh treasury vault. Medicine check (25) revealed he was killed by a targeted psychic damage spell designed specifically to kill, approximately 1.5 months before discovery. He was also severely starved and exhausted at time of death. A forged confession note (different handwriting to his actual journals) was planted on his body. A cracked-open cocoon was attached to the vault wall — identified as the remnant of a medium-sized shapeshifting fey creature that broke out after completing a transformation. The shapeshifter operated as treasurer for weeks, systematically draining the vault. Thori's ledger (decoded in Session 6) revealed he had been channelling public funds to the Crooked Men.

---

## The Red Hand Arrives in Lowmarsh (Session 5)

The Red Hand — a fanatical southern order of fey-hunters and rogue-witch hunters who follow "the Burning God" — entered Lowmarsh in force. Their leader Alexander Dunos imposed a full lockdown: silver-rod testing for every citizen (forces shapeshifters near their true form), and execution on sight for anyone who leaves. The shapeshifter can hold a new form indefinitely but requires a full day to change.

## The Sunderance — First Full Definition (Session 6)

Alexander Dunos revealed that the shapeshifter in Lowmarsh had calculated when the next Sunderance will occur. The Sunderance was formally defined: a global event where forests and glades physically change place, marking the end of one age and the beginning of another. It is catastrophic in scale and occurs roughly every 300 years. The current age is 352 years old, making it significantly overdue. If the wrong Fey factions learn the precise timing, it "could spell the end of the Glades."

---

## Merida's Mother — "The Rot Queen" (Session 6)

Merida's mother is known as "the Rot Queen" — a figure powerful enough to make a hag flee on sight upon hearing the name. The hag said: "I would not dare speak with the spawn of the Rot Queen." Merida learned the Darkwood/Hag tongue from her mother. Her mother was previously described as "a woman who did her own dealings and turned into a witch."

## The Winter Express (Session 7)

A magical train that travels through the Underdark (called "the Under") connecting Lowmarsh to Rymes. Operated by a skeleton Conductor in a blue suit. Tracks materialise moments before the train arrives. Each passenger finds a pre-punched ticket in their pocket with cryptic letters. The train submerges underground through Dwarven cave systems at enormous speed. Passengers see glimpses of Dwarven cities, crystal caverns, and underground farms.

---

## The Under / Underdark (Session 7)

A vast cave system beneath the Greenwoods. Fey rules still apply near some water sources. Races include: Dwarves, Deep Elves, Stone-skin Goblins, Blinders, Shades. Major cities are accessible via great elevators. The Winter Express is one transit method.

---

## The Winter Giver (Session 7)

A figure analogous to Father Christmas — jolly old man in a red cloak with a white beard, flying a reindeer sleigh (one reindeer has a glowing red nose). By lore (religion check), he is also a very evil spirit that devours and punishes the wicked. A second demonic face briefly appeared at the back of his head before he departed.

## The Stranger / The Herald (Session 9)

A deity appearing as a very large four-eyed raven. Also known as the Herald or Chooser of Fates. Associated with prophecy, heroes, death, and the choosing of fate. Functions as a Chooser of Fates, selecting those not yet ready to die. Appears in dreams to those marked for heroic destiny. The blind seer Zara at Ersatz University identified Merida's prophetic visions as visitations from The Stranger and recommended visiting the black-stone ziggurat at Goback to amplify contact.

---

## Goback's Black Ziggurat (Session 9)

The town of Goback contains a massive black-stone ziggurat said to amplify mortal visions and facilitate direct communication with gods. The blind seer Zara has visited it herself many times. This is the recommended site for pursuing prophetic abilities.

---

## The Order of the Eleventh Eye — Mirror Shard (Session 9)

A small mirror shard (3cm × 5cm) was found in the Rymes catacombs, wrapped in paper bearing the owl symbol of the Order of the Eleventh Eye. The inscription read: "Dean Stus — we leave this in your capable hands." When examined, the shard reflected a vague humanoid figure — dark hair, possibly female, standing behind the viewer's reflection. Physical contact caused disproportionate wounds. This is the first mirror-shard encounter in the campaign.

## The Tomb of the Horned King — Discovery (Session 10)

Beneath the Rymes catacombs, the party discovered the Tomb of the Horned King — an enormous carved deer skull (~20 ft tall, 20 ft wide horns) with green fog seeping from the eye sockets. The inscription reads: "Be gone from hence, for it is the last resting place of the Blighted Lord of Pestilence, the Prince of Rot, the Final Decay, the Green Lord of Arcadia. Hard was his death and too easy would come his return. Servants of all that is good, descendants of the free folk, guard this door at the Turning of the World and the Crossing of the Spheres. Weep, weep, and despair — for you look upon the Tomb of the Horned King." A keyhole is visible; the door only opens at a Sunderance.

---

## Bonekeeper's Guild — Original Purpose (Session 10)

The Guild's original founding charter was found in the Tomb antechamber. It reveals the Guild was originally a religious anti-undead order, specifically charged with imprisoning the remains of beings too evil to be allowed burial — the Horned King explicitly named. The modern Guild (funeral/burial service) has drifted far from this founding purpose. The charter was lost in the chaos of a prior Sunderance.

## Nimue's Dual Nature — Corrupted and Trapped (Session 11)

During the Observatory battle, Drizella nearly died and entered the liminal space between life and death. There, Nimue's true self — peaceful, drowning, trapped within her corrupted outer shell — communicated via Message spell: "There is a way you can stop me. The Waters of Pendragon can free me." This confirms Nimue is under magical compulsion (not acting freely) and that the cure lies at Pendragon. She also carries the title "Lady of the Lake."

---

## The Three Sister Swords (Session 11)

Sibylla identified Caliburn's scabbard in the wreckage of Nimue's divination machine. Through occultism, she learned that three legendary sister swords exist: **Excalibur** (currently in an anvil at Pendragon), **Caliburn** (missing; scabbard recovered; gifted by Merlin to Nimue after the Strife of Camlin), and a **third unnamed sword** (fate unknown). Nimue held Caliburn as Lady of the Lake but the sword itself was no longer in the scabbard.

---

## The Reaper — Personal Intervention (Session 11)

The Reaper appeared personally to dying Drizella in the liminal space between life and death. He honoured her service to the Bonekeeper's Guild (from Session 10) and offered her the choice to remain among the living: "If nothing changes we will be travelling together soon. You've done me a great favour, but if you wish to wait, I have all the time left in the world." This establishes the Reaper as an active, conversational deity rather than a passive force.

---

## Dean Marikosa — 900 Years Old? (Session 10)

In the catacombs, a preserved severed head labelled "Dean Maricosa" was found — but it was a very old human man with a beard, not the dragonborn woman the party knows. The nameplate dates to approximately year 205 of the Fifth Era (~900 years ago). Her name was also found magically erased from the faculty records at Ersatz University, hidden between Deans Stus and Jack. The current Dean Marikosa has held the position for nearly 80 years.
