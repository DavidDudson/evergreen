# Memphis Cadwallader

**Also known as:** Rumpelstiltskin, Tobias Gold, Louis Cypher, The Imp, The Fairy Godmother
**Status:** Alive
**Nature:** Devil (possibly *the* devil of this world)
**Alignment:** Chaotic — operates entirely on his own interests

---

## Appearance

In his most common form: a short gnome (approximately three feet tall), dressed in a fine brown coat with polished gold buttons bearing the crest of Avalon (two sheaths of wheat). Warm smile. Never in a hurry.

True form: an entity of pure flame — tail, horns, and hundreds of bound contracts burning in his wake.

He has an aversion to silver. He cannot speak his own name "Rumpelstiltskin" — it is magically shielded, presumably as part of a deal he made long ago.

---

## History

Memphis Cadwallader has been operating under false identities for at least three hundred years.

As **Tobias Gold**, he made the deal with **Briar Rose** ~300 years ago: her death would be postponed indefinitely in exchange for a future unspecified favour. That favour is still outstanding.

He was formerly **Queen Maeve's agent**, tasked with acquiring the Looking Glass, the Staff of Beguiling, and the Ring of Objectification. He failed on all three counts. His relationship with Maeve is now adversarial.

In the present day (Session 17), the Mythmakers Union signed a contract with him. The terms are presumably favourable to Cadwallader in the long run.

His heraldry (two sheaths of wheat) matches the crest of Avalon — the connection to Maeve's kingdom is unexplained.

Drizella suspects he facilitated the **Glass Slipper curse** on the Tremaine family, potentially as a favour to Lady Tremaine or as a side deal.

## Abilities

- Silence (magical)
- Time stop
- Dimension door
- Mind reading
- Contract magic (binding and unbreakable deals)
- Identity shapeshifting (maintains aliases across centuries)

---

## The Deals

Cadwallader deals in futures, not presents. His contracts always have a loophole — but he is the only one who knows where it is. He does not break contracts. He also does not explain what the contracts mean until it is too late to matter.

Known outstanding contracts:
- **Briar Rose**: the 300-year-old debt; the favour remains uncollected
- **The Mythmakers Union**: signed in Session 17; terms not fully disclosed

## Dialogue Notes

Voice: warm, paternal, unhurried. He speaks like a man who has all the time in the world, because he does. He finds genuine delight in clever wordplay and is never rude. He is also never, ever telling you the full truth.

He will not speak his true name aloud. If pressed on it he changes the subject elegantly. He refers to himself in the third person occasionally — "Cadwallader has found..." — which is unnerving.

His tells:
- When he is genuinely surprised, his coat buttons seem to glow brighter for a moment
- He taps the contract he's holding whenever someone asks about Briar Rose
