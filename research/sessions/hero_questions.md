# Hero Questions — EvergreenNova

Chronological record of hero questions, backstory prompts, and moral dilemmas posed by the DM across all 66 sessions. The mechanic was never fully formalised as alignment-granting multiple-choice — it functions primarily as backstory/characterisation prompts for hero point opportunities.

## Organic Moral Dilemmas (No Structured Question)

### Session 4 — Kill Lewis Short? Kill plague victims?
Shadic Brethren demanded Lewis Short's assassination. Drizella refused. Bigby executed a fully rabid goblin on medical grounds. No alignment effect.

### Session 5 — Serve democratic order or a god's agenda?
Party refused to kill Lewis Short for the Shadic Brethren. The Brethren's parting words: "Had Lewis Short died earlier today there may have been a great victory for our cause."

### Session 19 — "Can you save everyone?" (Galehaut)
During Mordred's knighting flashback, Galehaut says: "You can't save everyone." Implicit heroism vs. pragmatism tension. No structured choice.

### Sessions 20–21 — Free imprisoned creatures or wait for port?
Party debated freeing Mor'du and fey creatures from the Jolly Roger (crashing the ship) vs. waiting for a safe landing. Chose compassion; freed all creatures.

### Session 23 — Pacifism vs. violence for survival
Drizella's pacifism vs. necessity of killing Cobalt Scouts. Also: whether to accept Morgana's healing power (moral cost). No structured question.

### Session 26 — Negotiate with a demon or destroy it?
Party chose negotiation/grace over violence with the infernal fey beneath Mimsy's church. **Only session with a documented mechanical reward for a moral choice:** divine blessing (+20 temp HP, Resistance 5 to fire/evil/void/spirit, +1 persuasion).

## Unique Mechanics

### Session 1 — The Counting Rhyme (Prophetic d20)
Each player rolled d20; received a prophetic line. DM said numbers would be "extremely important in about a year." Not a hero question but a campaign-defining mechanic. Player rolls: Rose=6, Ryan=19, Emma=11, others=15, 4, 1.

### Sessions 65–66 — The Fool's Rock-Paper-Scissors
The Fool (god of joy/death) offered Briar a literal rock-paper-scissors game for survival. Briar lost → died permanently. Accepted the Fool's Deal in Session 66 → returned from death; must sleep in 5 dragon lairs; vomited blacksmith's tongs (random element).
