# Session 1 Summary

**Date:** 29 Aug 2023
**YouTube:** [Session 1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=veYpJwwx2yQ)
**Arc:** Arc 1 — Lowmarsh (Sessions 1–6)

## Synopsis

Six player characters independently arrive in Lowmarsh, a small democratically-governed clearing south of Camelot. The town is screening arrivals for a disease called Red Eye, which originated in the city of Xander (Xyander). Mordred and Drizella arrive together by caravan, along with Merida and Bigby. Sibylla and Nik Jones are already in town, jailed — Sibylla for theft after emerging from the Broceliande forest having lost her magic, and Nik for cheating at cards.

Drizella causes a scene at the gatehouse by casting Ancient Dust on the medical examiners rather than reveal her face, but is ultimately cleared and directed to the Sage Hospice. There she discovers that dozens of wounded Camelot soldiers are dying from Ghoul Fever — a disease spread by ghouls that causes victims to rise as undead if they die. With a critical medicine check (28), she determines that a live ghoul must be captured to extract a cure.

Meanwhile, Bigby visits Mayor Lewis Short at the town hall and is hired to investigate the disappearance of the town treasurer, Thori Black (a dwarf who kept the financial records in Dwarvish). Sibylla receives a direct visitation from her patron, the Three-Faced Lady, who manifests through her cat familiar with three distinct personalities and tasks her with heading east into the woods to find a ritual. Nik Jones experiences a supernatural vision — transported to a seabed of shipwrecks where a barnacle-covered spectral figure (later identified as Davy Jones) gives him a cryptic warning and an unknown object.

The session ends with all six PCs assembled and heading out of Lowmarsh's eastern gate toward a battlefield to the northeast, tasked with capturing a live ghoul to save the soldiers.

Rolls: Rose=6 ("gold"), Ryan=19 ("the oncoming formless wreck"), Emma=11 ("a key to the spectral door"), one player=15 ("a speech no tongue can teach"), one=4, one=1 ("once upon a time")
- **Red Eye screening** at the Lowmarsh gatehouse — all arrivals checked for the disease
- **Drizella's gatehouse incident**: casts Ancient Dust on medical examiners, intimidates guards (dirty 20), is examined privately and cleared
- **Ghoul Fever diagnosis**: Drizella identifies the soldiers' illness as Ghoul Fever (medicine check 28, critical success) and determines the cure requires a live ghoul
- **Thori Black missing**: Bigby is hired by Mayor Lewis Short to investigate the missing town treasurer
- **The Three-Faced Lady manifests** to Sibylla through her cat familiar — three heads (young/sympathetic, middle/pragmatic, eldest/harsh: "You bloody fool") task her with heading east to find a ritual
- **Nik Jones's vision**: a supernatural visitation from a barnacle-covered spectral figure on a seabed of shipwrecks — gives Nik something and warns "Don't let me find you... too far"

## Characters Introduced/Appearing

### Player Characters
- **Mordred** — 16-17 years old, scruffy black hair, pallid skin, ragged clothing with an embroidered cloak (tiny connected images on the inside). Quiet, travels with Drizella.
- **Drizella Tremaine** — ~23, heavy dark cloak with deep hood. Translucent skin showing muscle and bone. Green eyes that glow sickly yellow. White straggly hair. Casts Ancient Dust (necrotic cone). Seeks cover at the hospice's masked order of medics.
- **Merida** — Wild red hair, fur-lined cloak. From the north (near Mercia). Good with animals, noble bearing. Training under the Witch of the Woods to learn magic. Told a pivotal magical event is coming at the winter solstice.
- **Bigby** (Big B) — Human barbarian who can shapeshift into a wolf. Detective. "Fights for the rights of workers." Bad reputation from the Hogson Brothers incident ("eating pigs"). From Xander/Xyander.
- **Sibylla** (Sevilla/Sabil in captions) — Gnome witch, ~90 years old but appears ~20. Red hair (formerly grey). Patron: the Three-Faced Lady. Lost her magical abilities, was arrested for theft after leaving the Broceliande. Has a cat familiar.
- **Nik Jones** (Jones Nyx) — 21-22, ~5'3". Grew up with pirates. Ear-length brown hair, white linen shirt, leather waistcoat, signature black leather jacket. Deliberately underestimates himself. Jailed for cheating at cards. Father was a trader.

### NPCs
- **Lewis Short** — Mayor/Chairman of Lowmarsh's People's Committee. Older elf with a big moustache. Hires Bigby to investigate the missing treasurer.
- **Officer Bradley** (also rendered as Randall) — Law enforcement at the Broken Bricks guardhouse. "Great big brick of a man" in plate armour. Mentions scouts from the south.
- **Matron Simone** (Samoa in captions) — Head of the Sage Hospice. Wears a plague mask wrapped in chains of flowers and herbs. Directs the medical response.
- **Thori Black** (Sorry Black in captions) — Missing town treasurer. Dwarf. Older (late 60s equivalent). Kept records in Dwarvish. Previously banished from somewhere called "Silvari"(?).
- **The Three-Faced Lady** — Sibylla's patron deity. Elegant woman with three heads: young (sympathetic), middle-aged (pragmatic), elderly (harsh). Manifests through the cat familiar.
- **Davy Jones** (The Caretaker in captions) — Spectral figure who appears to Nik. Barnacle-covered, waterlogged, one recessed eye. Appears amid shipwrecks and skeletons. Gives Nik an unknown object.
- **The Witch of the Woods** — Merida's mentor. A witch (not a hag). Told Merida about the upcoming winter solstice and another witch who needs help.

## Lore Reveals

- **The Counting Rhyme (1–21)**: A prophetic verse where each number corresponds to a mystical concept. Each player's d20 roll will become significant later in the campaign. This is the first instance of the DM's long-range foreshadowing mechanic.
- **Red Eye disease**: A plague originating in Xander/Xyander. Causes flu-like symptoms for weeks, then affects the mind. All arrivals at Lowmarsh are screened.
- **Ghoul Fever**: A disease spread by ghouls. If victims die from it, they rise as undead. Curable by extracting fluid from a live ghoul (like a spinal tap).
- **Evergreen's geography**: "A world of forests and clearings." Travel between clearings is extremely dangerous; people move in large caravans with hired guards (Woodland Watch).
- **The basilisk statue**: In Lowmarsh's town square, commemorating a hero who fought a basilisk 300 years ago (dated Fourth Era, before the last Sunderance).
- **Witches vs. Hags**: The DM draws a distinction — witches are a "job" (practitioners), while hags "live in the wild, kidnap people, have abandoned society or have been warped by magical accidents."
- **Magic is tied to cosmic cycles**: Celestial events (solstices, equinoxes) create periods of heightened magical power.
- **Scouts from the south**: Officer Bradley mentions rumours of scouts approaching from the south — an early hint of the military/political tensions.
