# Session 9 Summary

**Date:** Feb 2024
**YouTube:** [Session 9](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FhOyfyV0nAo)
**Arc:** Arc 2 — The Winter Express / Rymes (Sessions 7–12)

## Synopsis

The session opens with hero questions and a recap establishing the party in Rymes, where they have already met Dean Marikosa, Memphis Cadwallader, and the changeling scholar Savvan, whose Parallax ritual is set for tonight. With the full day free, the party pursues two threads: Merida visits the blind diviner Zara at Ersatz University's School of Divination, and the rest of the party accepts the Bonekeeper's Guild's commission to investigate undead rising from the city's ancient sealed crypts.

Zara reads Merida's prophetic raven-dreams and identifies the four-eyed raven as **The Stranger** — also known as the Herald, the Chooser of Fates, a deity of prophecy and heroic destiny. She advises Merida to seek a massive black-stone ziggurat in the town of **Goback** to amplify contact with this god. Her parting warning — *"Stay away from the purple"* — is filed away by the party. Guild head **Enoch** briefs the party: the dead have been rising from the lowest crypt levels with "increasing vigour" unlike any natural cause. The Guild's members are forbidden from harming the dead, making them entirely useless against them. He gives the party a partial map from the university archives and a handful of healing potions.

The descent through seven or eight underground levels takes the party past graves from multiple previous ages. In the deep levels they trigger a Shrieking Flower — a large purple plant that shrieks deafeningly — which retrospectively explains Zara's warning. They fight through a guard of twisted, contorted skeletons with green-glowing eyes, and encounter the ghost of **Marabella**, an Ersatz University student who accidentally teleported herself into a sealed ancient chamber weeks ago and could not escape. She warns of something very old and very bad deeper in the tomb, then the party performs funerary rites to partially settle her spirit.

The session's most significant discoveries come in a jar-room and a pair of steel chests. The jar-room contains the preserved severed heads of every past head of Ersatz University — including one labelled "Dean Maricosa" which is clearly a very old human man, not the dragonborn woman they know. The chest yields a Librarian's Staff and, most importantly, a small mirror shard belonging to the **Order of the Eleventh Eye**, wrapped in paper bearing their owl symbol and addressed to a former dean. The shard wounds disproportionately on contact and shows a dark-haired woman standing behind the viewer's reflection. The session ends on a cliffhanger: a bound Undead Lord in broken-horned ceremonial armour, chained with ancient cleric runes in the deepest accessible room, taunts the party, sings a Necral lullaby about hearts stopping, and mentally compels a party member to drive a dagger into another's back.

## Characters Introduced/Appearing

### New NPCs

- **Zara / Zora** — Elderly blind diviner at Ersatz University's School of Divination. Large curly hair, big spectacles on chains, sits quietly observing students. Reads Merida's prophetic dreams and identifies the four-eyed raven as The Stranger (the Herald). Recommends the Goback ziggurat as a site to amplify divine contact. Cryptically warns: "Stay away from the purple."
- **Marabella** — Deceased Ersatz University student found deep in the crypt. Was practicing teleportation and accidentally transported herself into a sealed ancient chamber; could not escape and died. Her corpse wears wizard robes and clutches a textbook and dagger; her ghost lingers blue-glowing. Warns the party about the deeper evil and is partially put to rest through funerary rites. Cannot leave until her unfinished business (the root cause) is resolved.
- **The Undead Lord** — Powerful bound undead wearing ceremonial green-and-gold leaf-patterned armour with a broken-horned helmet. Chained in place with iron manacles inscribed with ancient cleric spells "full of hate." Speaks Necril and Common. Warns the party his master is waking below. Attempts mental compulsion on Drizella (partial success) and at session end sings a Necral lullaby before compelling a party member to strike another.
- **Dean Stus** (historical, deceased) — A former head of Ersatz University whose very old preserved severed head is found in the jar-room. His personal effects included the mirror shard wrapped in the Order of the Eleventh Eye's symbol.
- **Dean Walter** (historical) — The dean who succeeded Dean Stus. Their journal documents finding Stus's hidden personal effects (the mirror shard) in the dean's office, and appears troubled or embarrassed by the discovery.

### Returning NPCs

- **Enoch** — Head of the Bonekeeper's Guild in Rymes. Tall, very still, calm. Briefs the party on the undead crisis, provides the partial map and potions, and sends them into the sealed lower crypt. First substantive alliance with the party.
- **Memphis Cadwallader** — Wealthy fixer. His commission to find the Staff of Enchantment is now explicitly linked to the Order of the Eleventh Eye — the GM confirms the order appointed themselves as guardians of the staffs of power long ago.
- **Savvan** — Changeling scholar. Parallax ritual is tonight; the party has not yet addressed this thread. Time is running short.
- **Dean Marikosa** — Head of Ersatz University. Her identity becomes suspect: the jar-room contains a very old head labelled "Dean Maricosa" that is a human male, not a dragonborn woman. The current dean may be a long-lived successor who inherited the name, or may be extraordinarily ancient.

### PC Details

- **Merida** — Prophetic raven dreams confirmed as visitations from The Stranger (the Herald/Chooser of Fates). Given a clear directive: travel to Goback's black-stone ziggurat.
- **Bigby** — Has a stealth-capable cat familiar named Sean. Strips the +1 striking rune from the silver longsword for his own weapon.
- **Drizella** — Speaks Necril; attempts to negotiate with the undead rather than fight. Takes a philosophical stance that chained undead who cannot harm anyone should not be attacked without reason. Is partially mentally compelled by the Undead Lord.
- **Nik Jones** — Takes the Quicksilver Mutagen. First to feel the disproportionate wound from the mirror shard.

## Faction Developments

- **Bonekeeper's Guild** — First active alliance with the party. Enoch confirms the rising dead represent an unprecedented threat the Guild cannot address themselves due to their charter forbidding harm to the dead. The Guild's founding charter was from a city that no longer exists; their records are incomplete.
- **Order of the Eleventh Eye** — First appearance of their symbol in a gameplay-relevant context. A mirror shard left in the custody of a former university dean was wrapped in their owl sigil with the inscription "we leave this in your capable hands." The GM explicitly confirms the Order appointed themselves as guardians of the staffs of power long ago. Their connection to Ersatz University's administration runs at least 900 years deep.
- **Ersatz University** — The jar-room reveals all past university heads had their severed heads preserved in the lower crypt, raising profound questions about the institution's relationship to necromancy and the power waking below. The "Dean Maricosa" head is ancient and human; the current dean may be either centuries old or inheriting the name.

## Lore Reveals

- **The Stranger (the Herald)** — A deity associated with prophecy, heroic destiny, and the choosing of fates. Appears as a very large four-eyed raven in dreams. Functions as a Chooser of Fates: selecting those not yet ready to die and shepherding them. First named identification in the campaign.
- **Goback** — A town containing a massive black-stone ziggurat that amplifies mortal visions and facilitates direct communication with gods. First named as a significant location; aligns with the Arc 2 caravan destination and later Arc 4/5 events.
- **The Sunderance cycle** — The Bonekeeper's Guild's original founding city was destroyed in one of the periodic Sunderances. The lower crypt predates the current city of Rymes and possibly predates the previous Sunderance. Sunderances recur every 250–300 years.
- **Order of the Eleventh Eye and the university** — The order left a mirror shard in the custody of a former university head with the note "we leave this in your capable hands," establishing a long-term institutional relationship. They also guard the staffs of power. Their owl symbol appears here for the first time as a plot-relevant artefact.
- **The preserved deans** — All past heads of Ersatz University have their severed heads preserved in glass jars in the sealed lower crypt. The tradition is ancient and probably connected to the necromantic power waking below.
- **The mirror shard** — Extremely powerful and old. Bearing the Order of the Eleventh Eye's owl sigil, it inflicts stab-wound-severity injuries from a mere prick and reflects a dark-haired humanoid woman standing behind the viewer. This is the first mirror-shard encounter of the campaign; it seeds the major mirror-and-Bloody-Mary thread that runs through subsequent arcs.
- **The sealed tomb below** — Something ancient and powerful is waking in a deeper sealed chamber. The Undead Lord refers to his master waking below. Marabella saw a cracked tomb with something emerging. The undead rising throughout Rymes are symptoms of this awakening.
