# Session 4 Summary

**Date:** Oct 2023
**YouTube:** [Session 4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9ew-buXkOw)
**Arc:** Arc 1 — Lowmarsh (Sessions 1–6)

## Synopsis

The session opens with Sibylla responding alone to a crow summons, joining an old witch and a blue-skinned hag in a coven ritual in the woods northwest of Lowmarsh. Sibylla succeeds on an occultism check and receives a prophetic vision — a cluster of insect-like eyes with a great emerald centre to the northeast, a wooden chalice being consumed by rot to the southwest, a shadow skulking unseen through Lowmarsh, and a small metallic glint in the dirt. As she heads back to town, an old woman approaches from the north and reveals she too studied under Nimue, who has been missing some twenty years. She gives Sibylla a sealed letter with an unrecognised heraldic wax seal, says to speak her name to find her, but refuses to give it — then vanishes, leaving behind a rotting scarecrow with a jack-o'-lantern head. This woman is later understood to be Bessa of the Lone Pine.

The main party reconvenes at the Lowmarsh People's Committee session. They are formally thanked by Chairman Lewis Short and paid 75 silver for the ghoul work. Mordred requests the soldiers' share go to the families of the dead; Short agrees. The party then turns to the crop theft problem: Bigby's architectural knowledge identifies a hollow, fire-hardened section of the town wall concealing a tunnel, which farmer Paul Goodget smashes open with a hoe. Inside and beyond the tunnel, the party clears a small Red Eye-infected camp of goblins, a biting human, and a bugbear-type creature. Goodget pays the party and agrees to lend his safe key. Meanwhile a cloaked construct of solidified darkness — one of the Shadic Brethren — rises from the tunnel entrance and delivers two messages to Drizella: the timeline has changed (the party may now stay tonight and leave in the morning), and Lewis Short must die before day's end or he will "uncover things that should not be uncovered."

The session culminates in Thori Black's apartment, where Sibylla succeeds with a natural-20 hero-point reroll to dispel the illusion — confirmed as 8th-level occult magic, cast three weeks ago by or through a fey entity using a scroll or rite. The true state of the apartment is revealed: ransacked, mattress flipped, desk overturned, rotting food suggesting two to three weeks of abandonment. A prescription medicine bottle is found. Personal hygiene items are missing from the bathroom cabinet — suggesting Thori was taken with basic possessions or left under duress rather than in a fight. The session ends mid-investigation with the party still inside the apartment, the third vault key not yet retrieved, and Lewis Short's fate unresolved.

Nik Jones is also pulled mid-session into a seawater vision: he is at the bottom of the sea among shipwreck skeletons when Davy Jones appears, warns him not to die until it is his time, and speaks of "three" pursuing him. A great four-eyed bird grabs Nik's shirt and screams; Nik wakes dripping seawater, Frightened 2.

## Hero Question

No formal hero question this session. The central moral tension emerges organically through the Red Eye debate and the Shadic Brethren's assassination demand:

*"These infected goblins and humans are incurably diseased, violent, and suffering. The recognised medical treatment is death. But should we kill people who are victims of a disease for pay? And should we kill Lewis Short — a helpful civic leader — because a god's agents have told us to?"*

Drizella refuses to commit to killing Short. Bigby executes the chained fully-rabid goblin on grounds it is the merciful and medically sanctioned option. The broader debate is unresolved.

## Lore Reveals

- **Hags vs. witches** — In this setting, a witch is a human spellcaster; a hag is a witch (or other human) warped and transformed by overuse, misuse, or sustained magical exposure. Hags exist somewhere between human and fey. Sibylla herself may have undergone or reversed such a transformation (left ambiguous by the DM as a character choice).
- **Magic traditions** — Four confirmed traditions: Arcane, Occult (witches), Primal (rangers/druids), Divine (clerics). The Thori Black illusion was occult, not arcane; 8th level; cast via scroll or rite by a fey entity rather than directly by a powerful caster.
- **Covens** — Spellcasters and magical beings can form covens in groups of three for amplified power. Covens are especially powerful during a Parallax and full moon (double boost for divinatory magic).
- **Shadic Brethren — nature** — "Atronics": darkness incarnate; constructs without a physical body beneath their robes. Servants of the Faceless Soldier. Drizella's father had dealings with them. They work in dark magic, which is not inherently evil.
- **The Goodly Father / religious pantheon** — The Immaculate Church of the Goodly Fathers in Lowmarsh is an old-religion pagan temple, not an organised monotheistic institution. The same deity is known to sailors as "Davy Jones" and to others as "Uncle Mary" or "Mother Mary" — a sea god with multiple names. The church houses shrines to many other gods.
- **Red Eye disease** — Causes blood-red irises; victims become aggressive and eventually fully rabid. Standard treatment (per the Northern Medicas League handbook) is isolation and extermination if fully progressed. Drizella claims to have reversed it once; it requires a personalised potion using an item belonging to the infected person and a full day per individual to brew.
- **Lowmarsh town wall** — Several hundred years old, 4–4.5 metres high, base fire-hardened (double-baked earth, effectively terracotta) to prevent tunnelling — a standard anti-siege technique. Goodget's farms are the largest inside the walls, feeding most of the population.
