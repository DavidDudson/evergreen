# Session 5 Summary

**Date:** Oct 2023
**YouTube:** [Session 5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LAzA6OW_44I)
**Arc:** Arc 1 — Lowmarsh (Sessions 1–6)

## Synopsis

The session picks up where Session 4 left off: the party finishes searching Thori Black's glamour-dispelled apartment and then moves to the derelict Raggedy Chapel of the Heart directly opposite his window. A false priest answers the door — a Crooked Men operative disguised as a cleric of the Fool whose running makeup betrays him. After Drizella negotiates via the Message cantrip and 10 silver changes hands, the operative confirms what the party suspected: Thori Black is dead. The Crooked Men had hired an independent contractor to deal with him, then two days later Thori reappeared — meaning a shapeshifter took his place. The operative throws a smoke bomb and escapes; the party warns him the Red Hand is coming and recommends he leave town.

While Bigby retrieves the third vault key from the cheerful and entirely unsuspecting Oswald Burton at the lumber yard, he is attacked in the street by a disheveled dwarf assassin who strikes with a spring-loaded sleeve blade, deals significant damage, and escapes through the alleyways. The attack is unconnected to the shapeshifter plot — the dwarf was hired by enemies from Bigby's past. Bigby is treated at the hospice and the party presses on to open the vault.

Inside the treasury vault, they find Thori Black's body at a small writing table, a forged confession note in different handwriting, a cracked-open cocoon attached to the wall, and the treasury nearly emptied. A forensic examination places the time of death at approximately six weeks prior: Thori was killed by a targeted psychic spell designed to kill specifically, and was severely starved and exhausted at the time of death. The cocoon is identified as the remnant of a medium-sized shapeshifting fey creature that broke free after completing its transformation. The shapeshifter operated as treasurer for weeks, drained the vault systematically, and has since assumed a new form somewhere in Lowmarsh.

The session closes on two fronts simultaneously. The Red Hand march in force on Lowmarsh — soldiers in dull-red armour, robed spellcasters, and full-plate knights under the burn-scarred leader Alexander — and impose a silver-rod lockdown: every citizen must be tested, and anyone who flees will be executed on sight. The shapeshifter will appear near its true form under silver contact. Sibylla and Nik Jones, both at risk from the Red Hand's witch- and fey-hunting mandate, avoid the test this session. At the same session's end, Drizella prays to the Faceless Soldier and the Shadic Brethren respond: the Red Hand's arrival means the assassination of Lewis Short is now moot. "Had Lewis Short died earlier today there may have been a great victory for our cause. Now all will survive and none will be able to change."

## Hero Question

No formal hero question is posed this session. The closest moral tension is implicit and retrospective: the Shadic Brethren wanted the party to assassinate Lewis Short — a seemingly benign and cooperative civic leader — to protect their god's operations. The party chose not to kill him. The Brethren's parting words confirm that refusal had real consequences for their plans. The underlying question: *Do you serve order and democratic governance, or a shadowy god's agenda?* The party's answer was, in effect, the former — and the Brethren accept this and stand down.

## Faction Developments

- **Crooked Men** — Confirmed to have had a financial arrangement with Thori Black (he was skimming public funds for them). They hired an independent contractor to kill Black; the contractor apparently succeeded, but the shapeshifter then took Thori's form and the Crooked Men operated alongside it for weeks without realising. The false priest operative is not a killer; he claims he won't inform on the party but will not actively help. He is advised to leave town before the Red Hand arrives.
- **Shadic Brethren** — Confirm they wanted Lewis Short dead because he posed a threat to their operations. With the Red Hand's arrival pre-empting their plan, they stand down and announce contingencies in other towns. Their ultimate goal in Lowmarsh is now abandoned for this arc. Their parting words suggest the Parallax consequences are now diminished: "all will survive and none will be able to change."
- **Red Hand ("Burning God" order)** — Enters Lowmarsh in significant force: soldiers, robed mages, and full-plate knights. Imposes silver-rod testing and an execute-on-sight lockdown. Cooperates businesslike with the party investigation; Alexander is not antagonistic. Two internal wings identified: the **Red Dawn** (less proactive) and the **Red Hand** (active enforcers). Sibylla and Nik are specifically at risk from the witch- and fey-hunting mandate — both avoid the test.
- **People's Committee of Lowmarsh** — Treasury confirmed entirely emptied by the shapeshifter over weeks of systematic theft. Lewis Short is alive, warned, and grateful. The committee's public finances are in ruin.

- **C2. Find Thori Black** — Resolved. Thori Black is confirmed dead, killed approximately six weeks ago by targeted psychic magic from a medium-sized shapeshifting fey. Body found in the vault alongside a forged confession and a cracked cocoon. The treasury has been systematically emptied.

## Lore Reveals

- **The Fool as deity** — Identified as a divinity analogous to the Faceless Soldier and the Three-Faced Lady — another face of the same class of old-religion powers. Clerics of the Fool wear white makeup with black and blue patches that does not run (waterproof); the false priest's running makeup exposes him.
- **Fey and arcane magic** — Fey generally cannot use arcane magic; it requires booklearning and sustained practice they do not undertake. A fey who found an arcane scroll would more likely destroy it (explains the burned invisibility scroll found in Thori's apartment in Session 4).
- **Red Hand theology and methods** — A fanatical southern religious order: they hunt fey, track rogue witches, follow the Burning God, and refer to each other as "brother." Silver is the Burning God's holiest material; anything with a silver allergy is a "foul being." Their silver-rod test suppresses fey transformation, forcing shapeshifters near their true form. They are the closest analogue to an Inquisition in this setting — authoritarian methods, but not straightforwardly evil.
- **The shapeshifter's biology** — Can hold a new form indefinitely once assumed, but requires a full day in private to take on a new shape. The cocoon or sac is a by-product of the transformation process. The creature is medium-sized in its true form. It demonstrated fey intelligence: operated as treasurer for weeks, manipulated the Crooked Men, and systematically drained the treasury.
- **The Camelot/Avalon war** — Mentioned in passing as having been ongoing for approximately twenty years, suppressing tourism across the northern regions.
- **The Parallax** — Briefly recapped: a sixty-year alignment of the magical spheres that is now imminent. Multiple factions positioned around Lowmarsh because of it. The Red Hand confirms awareness of it. The Shadic Brethren's retreat suggests the window for their plans has now closed.
