# Session 21 Summary

**Date:** ~Oct 2024 (Arc 4)
**YouTube:** [Session 21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C16pvJeutbc)
**Arc:** Arc 4 — Sea / Jolly Roger / Goback (finale)

## Synopsis

The party breaks into the Jolly Roger's lower deck to free the imprisoned creatures. They find a leaked journal documenting **fey magic extraction** — powdered dryad tooth, fairy dust, leprechaun blood used for alchemical recipes and to power the ship. The cages contain sprites, fairies, a white **unicorn**, muscular creatures, and a **leprechaun** (high lord of the fey). A black oozing entity called **Fixit/Phiz** (one red eye, acidic) offers to eat through locks in exchange for freedom.

The party frees the creatures one by one. The leprechaun grants a sealed letter with a **wish-granting gold four-leaf clover** — one wish per person holding it, spoken aloud, but selfish wishes have unintended consequences. The unicorn grants a blessing (+5 unicorn points per character). Chaos pixies are released despite plans (immune to calm spells). Finally, the party pulls two safety levers simultaneously to release **Mor'du** (the gargantuan crocodile). The floor opens and they ride the crocodile through the sky as the Jolly Roger loses power.

Mor'du plunges into the sea, granting the party **24-hour water breathing**, and speeds them underwater to **Goback**. The crocodile speaks: "Violence isn't always the answer. Enjoy your lunch" — then swims back to hunt Captain Hook. Mor'du also reveals kinship with the Beast King: "They have the sense to free me... the hooked man shall no longer steal what belongs to the sea."

At Goback, the party finds an ancient city built around a massive **obsidian ziggurat**. The town was devastated by flooding decades ago; only a walled "new city" remains. **Silent monks** guard the ziggurat entrance, under magical Silence spells, growing increasingly strict under the **High Abbot Jor**. Entry rules: eyes must stay closed inside — "visions are only given to those who cannot see." Mordred convinces the monks to open the gates.

The **Looking Glass** (or The Stranger) has directed Mordred to find **Merlin**, who is alive but won't survive much longer. Dean Marikosa was the last person to see him.

## Characters Introduced/Appearing

- **Leprechaun** — Small fey, emerald robes, scythe-wielder. High lord of the fey. Grants wish-granting clover letter upon rescue.
- **Unicorn** — Pure white with horn. Grants blessing upon rescue.
- **Fixit/Phiz** — Black oozing entity with one red eye. Living acidic ooze. Eats locks and wood. Freed as ally; pursuing Hook for vengeance.
- **The Butcher** — Goback innkeeper/merchant. Provides local intel about the monks and town history.
- **High Abbot Jor** — Leader of the silent monks at the ziggurat. Serious and strong. Under his leadership, monks have become stricter and stranger.
- **Mor'du** (speaking) — Reveals himself as a primeval creature with kinship to the Beast King. Cannot be killed by natural elements (drowning, aging, falling). Can only be killed by weapons. Grants water breathing.

## Hero Question

No formal hero question. The central moral choice was freeing the imprisoned creatures (risking the ship crashing) — the party chose compassion.

- **The Stranger / The Looking Glass** — Directing Mordred to find Merlin. Confirmed as a divine entity communicating through the ziggurat and ship events.

## Lore Reveals

- **Primeval Creatures** — Cannot be killed by natural elements (drowning, aging, falling). Only weapons can kill them. Mor'du and the Beast King are kin.
- **Fey Magic Extraction** — Hook's journal documents systematic harvesting: powdered dryad tooth, fairy dust, leprechaun blood. Used for potions, healing, and powering the ship.
- **Goback History** — Ancient city predating the current ziggurat. Devastated by flooding ~20-70 years ago. Only a walled "new city" remains. Old ruins abandoned, forest reclaiming them.
- **Ziggurat Rules** — "Visions are only given to those who cannot see" — eyes must stay closed. Breaking the rule causes the magic to fail. Under the High Abbot, visions have become "more real" in recent years.
- **Walking Gods** — Gods who manifest in mortal form. The Magus is one of them (patron of wizards/scholars). The Faceless Soldier is associated with anonymity, hermits, outcasts.
- **Merlin** — Missing ~20 years. Alive but deteriorating. Dean Marikosa at Ersatz University was the last to see him.
