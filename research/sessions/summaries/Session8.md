# Session 8 Summary

**Date:** Jan 2024
**YouTube:** [Session 8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c0GpJkrBhxU)
**Arc:** Arc 2 — The Winter Express / Rymes (Sessions 7–12)

## Synopsis

The session opens immediately after the Winter Express arrives in Rymes. The Conductor's magical gifts from Session 7 are distributed among the party: a stuffed bear companion (can animate as a combatant), a coin of comfort (reduces fear conditions), a three-headed staff (additional damage types), and a telekinetic feather. The party rides the elevator up from the underground train station into Rymes — a huge proper city with a covered marketplace — and Memphis Cadwallader pays out the agreed escort fee (200 silver split six ways, approximately 35 silver each). The party is immediately confronted by an undead walker shambling through the marketplace, saying "the rise is coming." Drizella heals it briefly with Harm; it collapses and truly dies again. Father Lucas, a cleric in black robes with a collar of bones from the Bone Keepers Guild, appears and uses the Mind Conference spell to brief the party in a shared mental space: the undead are rising spontaneously from the lower catacombs without any known spell being cast, saying only "the rise is coming," and the Guild's creed forbids harming the dead, leaving them helpless. He directs the party to seek Enoch Hul, the Guild's guildmaster.

At the Sold Goat inn, Memphis has already set up a desk and written letters of introduction for all six party members. He formally proposes the Salasarglumm commission: the Beast King Carlos Vee, a disgraced Ersatz University wizard who botched a polymorph on himself and now rules Salasarglumm, holds three items stolen from the University — the Staff of Beguiling, the Ring of Objectification, and the Looking Glass (a powerful scrying ball). Memphis offers 500 silver for the Staff, 500 for the Ring, and 750 for the Looking Glass, plus two hours alone with the Looking Glass before delivery. He produces a formal contract; Nik reads it (legal lore, roll 25) and finds it broadly straightforward with some unusual clauses (no rhyming couplets — Queen Maeve is superstitious about attracting Fey; no buttering bread twice — a butler imp clause). Most PCs sign. Memphis also warns the party they may be approached by a rival contractor, a dwarf named Krennic Silver-Po, who travels with an albino woman with a bronze eye. The session's bulk is then spent at Ersatz University, as the whole party — including Savvan, who needs Observatory access for the Parallax — is admitted using Memphis's letters of introduction.

The University is far larger on the inside than outside, with Middle-Eastern-Hogwarts architecture, floating towers, animated silver-armour guards, and domed observatories. Dean Marikosa is revealed to be an ancient white dragon in black Regal robes — she has held the title of Dean for several hundred years. She examines Nik's blood at Drizella's prompting and a skull with curved horns rises from it, confirming infernal heritage: Nik's father is a demon currently on this plane who activated the blood-link when Nik began using magic involuntarily. Dean Marikosa also mentions a missing promising apprentice last seen practicing a teleportation spell with another student (Fosco Roic and Lindsay Hillis), and asks the party to find what remains of them — a hook that connects to the Bone Keepers' catacombs. She directs each PC to a specific faculty member.

The individual consultations each yield important personal lore. Merida consults Frond, a Leshii (half-plant scholar) in the University grounds, about the Great Beasts: Frond explains they are extensions of Titanic forces, likely called into being rather than naturally born, and that a curse tied to one will be very hard to break — the Ziggurat of Goback in the city of Goback is recommended as a place where the Gods' voices are amplified. Sibylla visits the library and head librarian Thr (very tall, completely grey bob haircut, jet-black eyes — also has infernal heritage) to obtain a partial map of the catacombs from an old University self-writing-map study, and reads up on witchcraft, learning she would need a mother-witch (the Cunning) to restore her power — her own mother, Inway, is still alive in self-imposed exile after a haggish succession scandal. Drizella delivers Simone's thesis to Asher in the Anatomy Department, then visits Kolen, an ancient hunched elf in a broom-closet office plastered in papers and string, who specialises in Fey research. Kolen explains that "Fairy Godmother" is a job description, not a name; they are drawn to mortals of powerful entitlement; to force one to appear, the party needs the token-anchor given to the Fey's original mortal patron (Drizella's sister) — which may be a glass slipper. Nik returns to Thr for a second consultation about his infernal parent: every time he uses his powers it pulls on the cord linking him to his demonic father, who is actively on this plane. Thr mentions a group called the Children of Falls/Fools who hold up in Goback and embrace their infernal nature — a possible resource. Session closes with all university consultations complete and the party about to make contact with Enoch Hul and the Bone Keepers Guild.

## Characters Introduced/Appearing

### New NPCs

- **Father Lucas** — Cleric of the Bone Keepers Guild (Reaper order). Slightly older man with greying hair and an elaborate black robe with a collar of bones. Was briefly a University student before being expelled following a "misunderstanding." Cast Mind Conference to privately brief the party about the rising undead. Polite, measured, anxious about public panic.
- **Dean Marikosa** — Ancient white dragon woman. Regal black robes fixed with a golden clasp. Very old appearance — loose skin, tired eyes — consistent with immense age. Has held the title of Dean for several hundred years. Runs Ersatz University, which is bigger on the inside than the outside. Has a grandfather clock in her office, a hovering globe of Evergreen, and a wardrobe that is apparently a small private library. Knowledgeable about the items stolen by the Beast King, about infernal bloodlines, and about the staff of beguiling as one of the Staffs of Power (old Arcane Empire relics).
- **Frond** — A Leshii (half-plant, half-person being). Lives in the University grounds; at first appears to be a shrub at the base of a large tree. Leafy and plant-like in appearance; straps themselves with leaves. Specialises in the Seven Great Beasts and Titanic arcana. Knows the Ziggurat of Goback. Northern-accented Evergreen regional knowledge.
- **Thr** — Head librarian at Ersatz University. Extremely tall; grey bobbed haircut; jet-black eyes (similar to Nick's). Also has infernal heritage — her demonic nature activated when she began studying magic at the University. Gave Nik meditation advice that did not work. Mentioned the Children of Falls (a group in Goback who embrace infernal nature). Speaks Diabolic.
- **Asher** — Faculty member in the Anatomy Department. Receives Simone's thesis on burns and oozes from Drizella. Pragmatic and busy; looking forward to the dissection lab.
- **Kolen** — Ancient hunched elf Fey researcher. Tiny broom-closet office three floors underground, walls covered in papers and string. Missing half an ear. Eccentric; initially mistakes Drizella for a prospective apprentice. Expert on the Fairy Godmother designation and the pact-token mechanism. Collects Fey teeth; would trade for a set of functioning ears.
- **Krennic Silver-Po** — Rival contractor, mentioned but not yet appearing. A dwarf. Travels with an albino woman with a bronze eye. Described by Memphis as an "old associate" who now works as a free agent. Memphis warns the party they will try to poach them with a higher offer.

### Returning NPCs

- **Memphis Cadwallader** — Pays the escort fee; formalises the Salasarglumm commission with a written contract; warns about Krennic Silver-Po; advises party to speak to Dean Marikosa about the stolen items. Produces champagne when the contract is signed.
- **Savvan** — Accompanies the party to the University to seek Observatory access for the Parallax. Nervous and unassuming. The party still knows little about them beyond that they carry large packs, have magical ability, and have some pressing personal business with the University.

### PC Details

- **Nik Jones** — Confirmed as having infernal blood (skull and horns appear in blood test). Father is a demon currently on this plane who activated the blood-link. Every spell use signals the father. Coin of comfort received from Conductor. Seeking control of his involuntary magic.
- **Drizella** — Delivers Simone's thesis; consults Kolen about her sister's Fairy Godmother pact; reveals the disfigurement to Kolen (who does not flinch). Also has a disembodied hand companion named Elder who helps mop the brow of Father Lucas during the Mind Conference.

- **Merida** — PC recap: looking for a cure for her mother and brothers. Consults Frond about the Great Beasts; learns a beast-conjured curse is very difficult to break; directed to the Ziggurat of Goback.
- **Bigby** — Buys better armour in the marketplace (hell breastplate, common item). Society check 28 on city knowledge. Questions whether the forest clearance is superstition (Craft check 12 — just superstition from his perspective).
- **Mordred** — PC recap: wants to solve a mystery big enough for validation and reputation. Mentioned as wanting to investigate the catacombs.

## Faction Developments

- **Bone Keepers Guild** — Introduced through Father Lucas. A dual organisation: clerical order of the Reaper and guild of undertakers/catacombs wardens. Creed forbids harming the dead, which leaves them powerless against the current crisis. Undead are rising spontaneously without a known spellwork source, saying "the rise is coming." Father Lucas serves as the street-level face; Enoch Hul is the guildmaster and further information-holder. The party has been directed to Enoch.
- **Queen Maeve / Avalon** — Commission formalised via formal contract. Items: Staff of Beguiling (one of the Staffs of Power, relics of the old Arcane Empire), Ring of Objectification, Looking Glass (temperamental scrying ball that "behaves as if it has a mind of its own," according to Dean Marikosa). Payment: 500/500/750 silver plus two hours' use of the Looking Glass. Contract clause: no use of items causes direct harm to uninvolved parties (added at Merida's insistence).
- **Ersatz University** — Major faction properly introduced. Bigger on the inside than outside; governed by an ancient white dragon posing as a long-serving Dean. The stolen items were University property (or held in trust for private collectors). The University has records on the catacombs and the Beast King. Several faculty members are now known contacts.
- **The Staffs of Power** — The Staff of Beguiling is confirmed as one of 10–12 ancient artifacts from the old Arcane Empire (the Staffs of Power, also called Stars of Old Arny). Some caused beguiling; at least one was a potent disintegration rod. Their origins are debated — possibly forged by the Magas or manifestations of pure magical will.
- **The Seven Great Beasts** — Introduced in detail by Frond. They are extensions of Titanic forces, called into existence rather than naturally born. Named beasts include: Vu (great beast of the hills), a Kraken (great beast of the seas), an unnamed giant crocodile (great beast of the shallows), a two-headed fire-breathing goat (beast of the mountains). Two are believed slain — a great lizard and one other. They are not straightforwardly mortal or immortal.

## Lore Reveals

- **Mind Conference spell** — Level 3 occult spell. Pauses apparent time for those included; allows telepathic group conversation without external observers noticing. Caster must maintain concentration; visibly taxing. Useful in ambushes as a non-damaging combat option.
- **The Bone Keepers Guild** — Dual organisation: undertakers and clerics of the Reaper (a death deity). Members include both skilled physicians and dedicated necromancy-wardens. Their creed forbids harming the dead. They originally came from a city swallowed by a previous Sunderance. Most pre-Seventh-Age records are lost.
- **The Sunderance** — Confirmed again: approximately every 250–350 years the Greenwoods expand and swallow cities. Currently ~358 years since the last one. The Bone Keepers' original home city was one casualty. Rymes recently cleared a large forest on its outskirts (possibly Sunderance-adjacent in timing).
- **The Staffs of Power / Stars of Old Arny** — 10–12 (accounts vary) ancient artifacts of the old Arcane Empire. The Staff of Beguiling is one; it was damaged during a Sunderance. Another is a disintegration rod. Their exact origins are unknown — possibly crafted by the Magas, or pure manifestations of magical will.
- **The Seven Great Beasts** — Named by Frond as extensions of Titanic forces, not naturally born. Known: Vu (hills), Kraken-type (seas), giant crocodile (shallows), two-headed fire goat (mountains), and at least two others (one a great lizard). Two are believed slain. They can be invoked in curses.
- **Infernal bloodlines** — Infernal heritage manifests as involuntary magic. The demonic parent "owns" the child's soul (not damnation — a formal claim). To break it: emancipate from the parent or kill the demon. Using infernal magic signals the parent across planes. The blood-link can be activated by either the child or the parent; if the child did not activate it, the parent reached out first.
- **The Haggish Succession** — Approximately 50 years ago, the All Mother of the hags died suddenly. Several candidates competed; Inway (Sibylla's mother) was briefly in charge, then resigned and left the order. Reason: she taught the Cunning to someone who should never have received it. She now walks the world in self-imposed exile.
- **Fairy Godmother mechanics** — Not a name, a job title. They are Fey drawn to mortals with powerful desire-fuelled entitlement; they strike pacts where the mortal gets what they want at a steep magical price. The mortal receives a pact token-anchor (ring, slipper, book, animal, etc.) that links them to the Fey. Getting the token allows a skilled Fey hunter to force the Fairy Godmother to manifest. Kolen collects Fey teeth as trophy/research specimens.
- **The Ziggurat of Goback** — A great structure of black stone in the city of Goback. Said to amplify the Gods' voices for those standing within. Can potentially enable direct communication with divine or Titanic forces. Recommended by both Frond (for Merida's curse) and Thr (in the context of Nik's infernal nature).
- **The Children of Falls** — A group based in Goback who embrace their infernal heritage rather than suppressing it. Thr was in contact with them during her time studying her own condition. May offer guidance to Nik.
