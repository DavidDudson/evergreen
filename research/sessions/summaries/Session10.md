# Session 10 Summary

**Date:** Mar 2024
**YouTube:** [Session 10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5LvAqMk7hK0)
**Arc:** Arc 2 — The Winter Express / Rymes (Sessions 7–12)

## Synopsis

The session opens mid-initiative, picking up directly from Session 9's cliffhanger in the deep catacombs beneath Rymes. The party battles the Zombie Lord and his horde of shambling undead — the green fog that pervades the chamber temporarily charms Merida, forcing her to fight her own companions. Mordred is brought to zero hit points and briefly "dies," glimpsing a vision of a vast dark forest unlike any in the Evergreen: enormous trees, dark creatures, and something immense made of bone and spite, crumpled and unable to move in the distance. Drizella talks to the Zombie Lord in Necril; it grudgingly admits the party is "worthy" and points them toward its master. Bigby decapitates it anyway ("He killed me. Yes, that was necessary.") — the severed head continues to sing an ominous Necral nursery rhyme about losing one's path in the dark woods.

Following the Zombie Lord's indication, the party travels another hour deeper through natural caverns and bricked-off side passages — evidence of deliberate sealing — before emerging into an enormous chamber. On the far wall: a carved deer skull twenty feet tall with twenty-foot horns, green fog seeping from the eye sockets, and a keyhole at its nose. Carved inscriptions in Alcane and Necril on both sides name this the **Tomb of the Horned King** — the Blighted Lord of Pestilence, Prince of Rot, Final Decay, and Green Lord of the vanished land of Arcadia. The door is magically sealed and cannot be forced. A side chamber holds a wooden key on a stone pedestal, guarded by a vine trap that kills Bigby's familiar Sean when he attempts to retrieve it.

The party regroups and makes several major discoveries: Marabella's spell notes are examined more closely and appear to have been **deliberately sabotaged** — her teleportation accident that breached the sealed passage may not have been accidental. They find the **original founding charter of the Bonekeeper's Guild** clutched in a skeleton at the antechamber, revealing the Guild was established as a religious anti-undead fighting order specifically charged with imprisoning the Horned King's remains. Bigby uses Architecture knowledge to identify a designed collapse point in the corridor; the party seals the passage with hammers, cutting off the source of the undead rising.

Returning to the surface, they collect their reward from Enoch (120 silver and the Blessing of the Reaper) and deliberately withhold the existence of the Tomb and its key. At Ersatz University they find the faculty hall board has a Dean Marikosa listed for nearly 80 years — but a concealed illusion spell hides the name "Dean Mara" scratched between Dean Stus and Dean Jack in records from ~550 years ago, erased with golden arcane script. The session ends on a second cliffhanger at the university's Observatory: instead of meeting Savvan for the Sunderance ritual, the party finds the **Deer-Skull Witch** (later revealed as Nimue) mid-ritual, floating green-glowing eyes surrounding her as she exploits the Parallax to power a necromantic ceremony. She turns, extends a hand, and Mordred critically fails his Will save — session ends on initiative rolled.

## Characters Introduced/Appearing

- **Marabella** (ghost, carried over from Session 9) — Her ghost is more fully explored this session; the party discovers her spell notes were likely sabotaged, implying her fatal teleportation accident was engineered rather than accidental. Her studies were under Dean Marikosa's supervision. The party lays her corpse to rest.

Concedes the party is "worthy" when Drizella addresses him in Necril, then points toward his master before Bigby decapitates him. The severed head continues singing.

Identified as the same cloaked deer-skull figure the party glimpsed after the battle of Camlin in Sessions 2–3. Her identity as Nimue is not yet confirmed to the party.

- **Dean Marikosa** — Not directly encountered; the faculty board investigation deepens the question of her identity. A prior "Dean Mara" with her name magically erased from records ~550 years ago suggests she may be extraordinarily ancient, or her name was inherited by a successor.

## Lore Reveals

- **The Horned King's titles**: Blighted Lord of Pestilence, Prince of Rot, Final Decay, Green Lord of Arcadia.
- **Arcadia**: The Horned King's domain. No longer exists. He was lord of Arcadia before being vanquished at the Fifth Age, approximately 600 years ago (in the 352nd year of the Seventh Age, this places his defeat at the end of the Fifth Era or opening of the Sixth).
- **The Tomb's seal**: Designed to open only "at the Turning of the World and the Crossing of the Spheres" — a Sunderance. The last Sunderance was 352 years ago; the next is significantly overdue (Savvan previously indicated 12–18 months away).
- **The Sunderance cycle**: Approximately every 250–300 years; the current one is overdue at 352 years since the last. The Horned King's tomb door was designed with the Sunderance as its trigger.
- **The Bonekeeper's Guild's founding purpose**: Originally a religious anti-undead fighting order specifically tasked with guarding the remains of entities too evil to be allowed burial — the Horned King the primary charge. This history was erased by a prior Sunderance; the modern Guild is an undertaking service that has entirely forgotten its origins.
- **Parallax**: An astronomical event occurring roughly twice monthly that amplifies magic significantly more than a regular night. Nimue is exploiting one for her Observatory ritual.
- **Mordred's death-vision**: When briefly at 0 HP near the Tomb, Mordred glimpsed a dark forest unlike anything in the Evergreen — giant trees, dark creatures, and something vast made of bone and spite, crumpled and unable to move in the distance. Almost certainly a psychic bleed-through from proximity to the Tomb, offering the party their first indirect glimpse of the Horned King's imprisoned domain.
- **Marabella's accident as sabotage**: Her teleportation spell notes appear to contain deliberate errors. Her accident that breached the sealed passage — and began the undead rising — may have been engineered. The saboteur's identity and motivation are unknown, but the university connection implies institutional involvement.
